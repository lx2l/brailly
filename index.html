Brailly

Brailly is a website that works as a kind of text translator to the Braille writing system, in a simple way.

What is Braille?

Braille is a tactile reading and writing system designed for people who are blind or visually impaired. It consists of a set of symbols representing letters, numbers and other characters, formed by combinations of raised dots arranged in a six-position cell (two columns and three rows).

What is its origin?

Braille was developed by Louis Braille, a young blind Frenchman, in the 19th century. Braille lost his sight at the age of three due to an accident, and in 1824, at the age of 15, he created this system based on a tactile writing method used by the French army called "night writing", which was used so that soldiers could communicate in the dark. Louis Braille simplified this system, making it more accessible and practical for everyday reading and writing.

How does it help us?

Braille allows blind people to read books, documents and other printed materials, providing access to information that might otherwise be inaccessible.

It facilitates the education of the visually impaired, enabling them to learn and participate in academic environments alongside their peers.

It promotes social and labor inclusion, as it allows blind people to integrate into society more effectively.

Learning Braille also contributes to the development of motor and cognitive skills, which improves the autonomy and confidence of blind people.
